About

With over 35 years of experience in Social Services, Cynthia has dedicated her life to helping others thrive. From Case Manager to Director of Programs, her journey has always been driven by a desire to uplift individuals and families, guiding them toward brighter futures. Cynthia's passion for service is rooted in her own story—one marked by resilience and a commitment to making a positive impact.

Born in New Haven, Connecticut, Cynthia moved to New York City, where she grew up in public housing. Looking up at the towering buildings around her, she often wondered, "What does life have to offer on the other side of that rainbow?" That curiosity fueled her lifelong commitment to helping others realize their potential.

Now semi-retired and living in Jacksonville, Florida, Cynthia channels her passion and experience into her Certified Life Coaching practice. Her mission is to provide personalized coaching services that support individuals in regaining and bolstering their confidence in life, work, skills, and education—enabling them to successfully pursue their goals. She specializes in personal development and overcoming obstacles, working primarily with young adults, women and families, and millennials navigating career decisions.

Are you ready to take control of your future and overcome your obstacles? Connect with Cynthia today to start building the life you envision!

Work

Cynthia credits her success to the unwavering support of her kind and loving family, which enabled her to earn two college degrees and build a thriving career in Social Services. With more than 35 years of experience, Cynthia has demonstrated a remarkable ability to lead, organize, and mobilize individuals and teams to make a meaningful impact on communities and individuals alike.

Early in her career, Cynthia held leadership roles within the American Red Cross, including Director of Emerging Services and Assistant Manager with the International Division. During this time, she supervised over 6,000 service members and managed 50 to 60 volunteers, showcasing her strength in leadership and community organization. Upon returning to the U.S., she continued her impactful work by leading programs that supported military installations and families.

Her professional journey then expanded to roles that directly addressed community needs and social challenges. As a Family Service Counselor and Supervisor, Court Liaison, Medical Disability Examiner, and Public Assistance Worker, Cynthia worked on the front lines of support for individuals and families facing hardship. She established policies, directed programs, and collaborated with schools, social services, hospitals, and community panels to deliver essential services.

Cynthia's expertise spans a wide range of areas, including foster care services, prison reentry programs, developmental disability services, public assistance, and child support advocacy. Whether helping individuals adapt back into the community after incarceration or guiding families through the complexities of social services, Cynthia has consistently demonstrated her commitment to empowering others with the tools and resources they need to succeed.

One of her greatest strengths lies in her ability to connect, communicate, and mobilize. From recruiting, hiring, and training professionals to developing community partnerships, she has continuously shown a talent for transforming challenges into opportunities. These skills directly inform her work as a Life Coach, where she leverages her vast experience to help clients regain confidence, develop practical life skills, and create attainable goals.

Success Stories

From Crisis to Triumph

Cynthia worked with a mother of six who faced legal challenges that put her children at risk of entering foster care. Through personalized coaching and goal setting, Cynthia empowered her to rebuild her life—starting from working as a fast-food cashier to ultimately becoming a manager. With Cynthia's support, she secured stable housing, completed an outpatient drug treatment program, had her court record sealed, and was reunited with her children. This remarkable transformation not only changed the mother's life but also gained local and national recognition for the client and the organization Cynthia represented.

An Academic Turnaround

During her time working in behavioral health services, Cynthia supported a high school senior whose mother was concerned about his mental health and academic performance. Facing the possibility of not graduating, he needed guidance and clear direction. Cynthia worked with him to identify his strengths—particularly in science and technology—and helped him set realistic, attainable goals. With her mentorship, his grades improved from C-level to A-level, he participated in college preparatory meetings, and ultimately graduated with a scholarship to attend vocational college.

"After so many years of providing platforms to assist individuals in their journey and receiving numerous awards and accolades, it's no wonder I became a Certified Life Coach."
